# Hyperfocus Mode — Deep Dive Recap

A full summary of everything built so far for the Hyperfocus Mode anime universe.

---

## Big Picture

**Title:** Hyperfocus Mode

**Core premise:** In Nexus City, a hyper-connected tech metropolis, a neurodivergent technopath named Lydrix can reorganise reality by reading the hidden systems inside matter and machines. His Hyperfocus Mode lets him stop city-level disasters, but it drains him mentally and physically. A hacker crew called The Static is trying to tear the city apart through The Grid, while Kat's off-grid workshop Make It Work becomes the only truly safe place — and Lydrix's new home.

**Main theme:**
> Being different is not the problem. Being forced to hide it is.

---

## Main Cast

### Lydrix

- Neurodivergent technopath, early 20s, lean build, quiet and sharp
- Wears dark tech shades because faces and eye contact overload him
- Always has earphones in to block city noise
- **Photographic memory:** sees every object and location as a mental snapshot
- Can recall exactly where things are and map entire systems from memory
- **Tidy bug:** clutter feels like broken code, so he constantly organises spaces including Kat's workshop
- Powers: senses tech and hidden systems, dismantles and rebuilds machines, hijacks nearby tech by touch, turns scrap into tools, drones, shields, and devices
- **Hyperfocus Mode:** world goes silent, patterns light up, memory becomes a 3D map; can rebuild failing infrastructure in seconds but crashes afterwards

### Kat

- Full name Katrina "Kat", owner of Make It Work
- Bold, blunt, caring, tells the truth even when it stings
- Practical workshop clothes, paint and dust everywhere, always doing something with her hands
- Multi-craft maker: painting, 3D printing, glass blowing, sculpting, repairs, prototypes
- Never says no to a new job even if she's never done it before — figures it out as she goes
- Constantly loses tools and asks Lydrix where things are — he answers instantly from memory
- Hates when Lydrix tidies her workshop even though it obviously helps
- Runs on cash and paper records only — no Grid connection, untouchable by hackers
- Falls asleep easily if she sits down after working too hard
- Does not understand code or AI

### Bolt

- Self-modified delivery droid who came in with a broken wheel and never left
- Small human-ish frame, mismatched parts, Kat's bright paint splashes and a lightning bolt on the chest
- Big round expressive eyes, short punchy speech style
- Takes every delivery extremely seriously like a life or death mission
- Runs on older firmware — The Static cannot properly hack or track Bolt
- Lydrix has quietly upgraded his systems, making Bolt the perfect off-Grid messenger and scout
- Puts a blanket on Kat when she falls asleep in the chair
- Secretly agrees with Lydrix about the workshop chaos but will never say this in front of Kat

## Villains — The Static

An organised hacker crew trying to collapse Nexus City by corrupting The Grid, then rebuilding the world their way through chaos. The dark mirror of Lydrix: they corrupt while he rebuilds.

### Static King — Leader
- Tall, still, long dark coat, visor with one glowing eye
- Power: system dominance — controls multiple city systems at once, triggers cascading failures
- Passive: Dead Signal — tech slows and glitches near him

### Glitch — Chaos Infiltrator
- Slim, neon streaked hair, mismatched hoodie with tech panels
- Power: signal fragmentation — clones signals, floods systems with fake alerts, ghost copies on cameras
- Passive: Noise Field — tracking attempts return corrupted data

### ByteWitch — Manipulator
- Stylish layered cyber fashion, screen-like eyes, wired jewellery
- Power: code possession — rewrites device behaviour, System Puppeteer turns Lydrix's creations against him
- Passive: Hex Signal — devices behave unpredictably near her before she touches them

### Null — Silent Saboteur
- Minimal black and grey clothes, plain mask, almost invisible
- Power: total digital erasure — creates blackout zones, blinds Lydrix's sensing ability
- Passive: zero digital footprint, invisible to cameras and tracking

### Overclock — Brute Force
- Big build, cyber-street gear, exposed mechanical limbs, heat shimmer
- Power: mechanical augmentation — super strength and speed, Redline overdrive burst
- Passive: Running Hot — nearby tech degrades and overheats

## World and Locations

### Nexus City and The Grid

- Dense, neon, loud, hyper-connected city where The Grid runs everything
- Managed by Nexus Corp with private security arm Grid Watch
- Nexus Corp secretly tracks data, movement, and technopaths
- The Grid is efficient and fragile — perfect target for The Static

### City Zones

| Zone | Description |
|---|---|
| The Core | Bright, crowded, glitch-prone central hub |
| The Sprawl | Messier outer districts, community-heavy, Kat's area |
| The Undercroft | Tunnels, old infrastructure, The Static's likely base |
| Signal Quarter | Tech and servers district, Static King and ByteWitch territory |
| Dead Zones | Grid-weak pockets, Null's map |

### Make It Work

- Off-Grid workshop in The Sprawl: cash only, paper records, no smart surfaces
- Interior: paint everywhere, glass pieces, 3D prints, tools on walls, sketches, Kat's sleep chair, Bolt's charging corner
- Kat's philosophy: never say no to a job
- Lydrix lives in the storage space upstairs
- The one place The Static and The Grid cannot directly touch

## Episode 1 Structure

### Act 1 — Overload and Sparks

1. **Cold open — minor glitch**
   - Brief anonymous Grid anomaly hinting at The Static before cutting away
   - Sets mood without explaining everything

2. **Slice of life overload**
   - Lydrix navigating The Core: crowds, noise, screens
   - We see the world as noise to him and as code underneath

3. **Kat's world contrasted**
   - Quick cut to Kat in Make It Work, covered in paint, working on a ridiculous job she's never done before
   - Bolt runs a delivery, we see their dynamic and how off-Grid the shop is

4. **Escalation**
   - Glitch and ByteWitch trigger a real city-level Grid cascade
   - Traffic deadlocks, bus almost crashes, doors and security systems misfire
   - Static King watching from the Signal Quarter, completely calm

### Act 2 — Hyperfocus and Choice

5. **Lydrix activation**
   - Lydrix drops into Hyperfocus Mode
   - Audio design flips: city noise mutes, UI-style overlays show patterns and broken links
   - He uses nearby scrap to rebuild a key junction of The Grid, stopping the disaster

6. **Aftermath**
   - People stare; some film him; a few mutter about freaks or illegal mods
   - He's exhausted, overwhelmed, and just walks away

7. **Kat sees him**
   - Via Bolt or an old non-Grid camera, Kat catches what he did
   - She is immediately interested rather than afraid

8. **Intersection**
   - Lydrix drifting in The Sprawl, avoiding The Core
   - Passes Make It Work just as Kat is mid-job
   - Bolt nearby. Kat clocks him as "that guy"

### Act 3 — New Home, Rising Threat

9. **The offer**
   - The perfect short scene: Kat offers the upstairs storage
   - Lydrix insists he is not moving in
   - Kat: "Sure, mate."
   - Bolt watches, silently approving

10. **Workshop chaos comedy button**
    - Kat losing a tool mid-sentence
    - Lydrix telling her exactly where it is without looking
    - Lydrix quietly tidying a section of the workshop
    - Kat: "I HAD A SYSTEM!"
    - Lydrix: "Your system was chaos."
    - Kat: "IT WAS MY CHAOS."

11. **Villain tag**
    - Cut to The Undercroft or Signal Quarter
    - The Static reviewing data on the technopath incident
    - Static King: this is not random. Lydrix becomes a target.

## Teaser Trailer Concept (45–60 seconds)

### Structure

**0–5s — Title and flashes**
- Black screen, glitching text: NEXUS CITY ONLINE → NEXUS CITY FAILING
- Smash cut to title: HYPERFOCUS MODE

**5–15s — Lydrix fragments**
- Shots of Lydrix walking through crowds, shades and headphones on
- Quick shot of his eyes glowing behind the shades as Hyperfocus Mode starts
- Voice-over (Lydrix, quiet):
  > "The city is too loud. But the systems… they're so clear."

**15–25s — Kat and the shop**
- Kat hammering, glass-blowing, 3D printing, laughing with a customer, covered in paint
- Exterior of Make It Work glowing warm in the Sprawl night
- Kat VO:
  > "You break it, I'll fix it. Or try. No refunds."

**25–35s — The Static threat**
- Quick flashes: Static King's glowing eye, Glitch laughing in static, ByteWitch in front of corrupted screens, Null in a blackout alley, Overclock cracking the street
- Glitch VO, distorted:
  > "Let's see what happens when the whole Grid goes dark."

**35–45s — Hyperfocus hero moment**
- The big shot: Lydrix in Hyperfocus surrounded by floating scrap and glowing circuit lines, rebuilding a collapsing street system
- Vehicles slam to a halt around him
- Kat watching, impressed
- Bolt rolling in with a package, slightly on fire
- Kat VO, amused:
  > "You look like you need somewhere to go, mate."

**45–55s — Emotional theme beat**
- Short montage:
  - Lydrix alone in his upstairs room looking at the city
  - Kat asleep in the chair, Bolt putting a blanket on her
  - Lydrix quietly rearranging chaotic shelves while Kat complains in the background
- Lydrix VO:
  > "I'm not broken. The system is."

**55–60s — Logo and tagline**
- Title card: HYPERFOCUS MODE
- Tagline:
  > "Rebuild the system. Or watch it burn."
- Final glitch of The Static logo over the city
