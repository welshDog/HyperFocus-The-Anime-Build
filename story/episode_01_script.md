## ACT ONE — OVERLOAD AND SPARKS

---

### SCENE 1 — COLD OPEN

**EXT. THE GRID — NEXUS CITY — NIGHT — DIGITAL SPACE**

*BLACK SCREEN.*

*A faint hum. Then a pulse. Data flows like water through a neon-lit grid of connected nodes — roads, buildings, systems, people.*

*Everything is working. Everything is perfect.*

*Then — a flicker.*

*One node. Just one. A tiny anomaly in Signal Quarter. A packet of data that shouldn't be there.*

*The camera pushes in on it. The anomaly pulses. Laughs, almost.*

*Then it disappears.*

*Like it was never there.*

**TITLE CARD — glitch effect:**
> **NEXUS CITY. POPULATION: 9.4 MILLION.**  
> **THE GRID UPTIME: 99.97%**  
> **ANOMALY LOG: 1 INCIDENT — CLASSIFIED**

*SMASH CUT TO:*

---

### SCENE 2 — THE CORE — LYDRIX IN THE CROWD

**EXT. THE CORE — MAIN BOULEVARD — MORNING**

*The city is a wall of noise and light. Giant screens blast adverts. Delivery drones buzz in tight formation. Hundreds of people flood the pavement.*

*For most people, this is Tuesday.*

*For **LYDRIX**, this is combat.*

*We see him from behind first — lean build, dark jacket, TECH SHADES over his eyes, EARPHONES in. He moves through the crowd like water around stones. Deliberate. Controlled. Head down.*

*Then the camera swings around to his face.*

*LYDRIX, early 20s. Quiet eyes behind the shades. Jaw tight. He's holding it together, but only just.*

*SUPER-IMPOSED over the scene — his perspective: every person has a faint aura of data. Every device has a visible signal. The city is screaming at him in a language only he can hear.*

**LYDRIX (V.O.)**  
Nexus City runs on The Grid. Every light. Every door. Every signal.  
Eight million people who never think about the systems keeping them alive.  

*He steps off the pavement to let a group of office workers rush past. He breathes.*

**LYDRIX (V.O.)**  
I think about them constantly.  
It's not a choice.

*He passes a building wall covered in screens — news cycles, adverts, social feeds. His eyes twitch. Too much input. He angles his head down.*

*Then — a bus in front of him. A woman drops her bag. Stuff spills everywhere.*

*Every person around her steps over it.*

*Lydrix stops. Bends down. Hands her the items back, one by one, without making eye contact.*

**WOMAN**  
Oh — thank you—

*He's already walking again. Earphones back in. Gone.*

---

### SCENE 3 — MAKE IT WORK — KAT'S WORLD

**INT. MAKE IT WORK WORKSHOP — THE SPRAWL — SAME TIME**

*CUT TO: warmth.*

*The workshop is glorious chaos. Tools on every wall. Paint everywhere — floor, ceiling, **KAT**'s face. A 3D printer hums in the corner. Half a glass sculpture sits in a vice. Someone's motorcycle engine is on the workbench next to a stack of children's toys.*

*KAT, mid-20s, sleeves rolled up, practical clothes covered in evidence of everything she's ever made. She's on the phone, writing on paper — actual paper — with an actual pen.*

**KAT**  
*(into phone)*  
Yeah, I can do that. What even is a pressure-reactive hinge? ...No, doesn't matter, I'll figure it out. Drop it off Thursday.  
*(beat)*  
Cash. Always cash.

*She hangs up. Looks at her notes. Circles "pressure-reactive hinge" and writes "look this up" next to it.*

*From the corner, **BOLT** rolls in. Small droid, human-ish frame, mismatched parts, a lightning bolt painted on the chest in Kat's signature yellow. Big round eyes that somehow manage to look excited.*

**BOLT**  
Delivery to Copper Lane: complete. No incidents.  
*(tiny pause)*  
Minor incident. Resolved.

**KAT**  
What kind of minor incident?

**BOLT**  
The package was briefly on fire. It is no longer on fire.

**KAT**  
*(not even looking up)*  
Was it on fire when it arrived?

**BOLT**  
Definition of "arrived" is flexible—

**KAT**  
Bolt.

**BOLT**  
No. It was not on fire when it arrived.

**KAT**  
That's all I needed.

*She gestures somewhere behind her.*

**KAT**  
Go plug in. You've got the Eastside run in an hour.

*Bolt rolls toward the charging corner — a cosy little nook Kat built for him, painted with lightning bolts. A small hand-written sign above it: "BOLT'S CORNER. KNOCK FIRST."*

*Bolt looks at it with visible pride every single time.*

---

### SCENE 4 — THE STATIC MOVES

**INT. THE UNDERCROFT — SIGNAL QUARTER JUNCTION — NIGHT**

*Underground. The walls are covered in illegal data feeds — live streams of every camera and sensor in the city. Everything Nexus Corp sees, The Static sees too.*

*GLITCH sits cross-legged on a server rack, neon-streaked hair flickering like a bad connection. She's grinning at a holoscreen full of cascading code.*

*BYTEWITCH stands behind her, arms folded, watching. Her wired jewellery catches the blue light. Screens nearby flicker just from her proximity.*

*In the shadows: NULL. Still. Near-invisible. Just a shape.*

*At the back: OVERCLOCK, massive, heat shimmer rising off his mechanical arms. Impatient.*

*And at the centre — seated, still, coat perfectly still in the airless room — **STATIC KING**. One glowing eye behind the visor. He's not watching the screens. He's thinking.*

**GLITCH**  
Okay, okay. I found a beautiful little crack in the Core transit hub. If I push a signal fork through the tertiary node — *(chef's kiss)* — cascades like dominoes.  
Buses. Lifts. Security doors. The whole junction becomes a lovely little disaster.

**OVERCLOCK**  
Do it. I'm bored.

**BYTEWITCH**  
*(dry)*  
You're always bored.

**OVERCLOCK**  
Because we keep planning instead of breaking things.

**STATIC KING**  
*(still not looking up)*  
This isn't breaking things.  
*(beat)*  
This is a test.

*Silence.*

**STATIC KING**  
The Grid is strong. The people trust it. Before we tear it down — we need to know how fast it fights back.  
*(finally looks at Glitch)*  
Do it. And watch what happens after.

*Glitch's grin widens.*

**GLITCH**  
With pleasure.

*Her fingers fly across the holoscreen. Code flows like water. She finds the crack and —*

*Pushes.*

## ACT TWO — HYPERFOCUS AND CHOICE

---

### SCENE 5 — THE CASCADE

**EXT. THE CORE — MAIN BOULEVARD — DAY**

*Everything is normal.*

*Then — a traffic light glitches. Goes red, green, then dead.*

*A delivery drone veers wildly and clips a building corner. Sparks.*

*Someone's phone screen goes black. Then someone else's. Then the shop displays.*

*A bus — fully loaded — lurches to a stop in the middle of the road. The doors won't open. Passengers inside start panicking, hammering the glass.*

*Security doors on a building slam SHUT randomly, trapping people half in, half out.*

*The crowd tips from confused to frightened.*

**CROWD MEMBER 1**  
What's happening?

**CROWD MEMBER 2**  
The Grid's down — my phone, everything—

**CROWD MEMBER 3**  
*(pointing at bus)*  
Someone's trapped in there!

*Chaos spreads outward from the junction like a wave.*

---

### SCENE 6 — LYDRIX ACTIVATES

**EXT. THE CORE — SAME STREET — CONTINUOUS**

*Lydrix is half a block away.*

*He stops.*

*Not because he heard the crowd — he can't, earphones in. He stops because he *felt* it. A system signal going wrong has a specific texture in his mind. Like a wrong note. Like a broken frequency.*

*He pulls out one earphone.*

*Looks up.*

*His perspective shifts — overlaid now with his tech-sense. The entire Grid around him lights up as a pattern. Red threads where signals are breaking. Black holes where systems just died. A spreading web of failure originating from three blocks ahead.*

**LYDRIX**  
*(quiet, to himself)*  
That's not a glitch. That's a push.*

*He starts moving. Not running — yet. He's reading the pattern as he walks, eyes scanning the environment, cataloguing every piece of material around him.*

*A pile of construction scrap on a corner.*  
*Torn signage.*  
*A broken service droid, abandoned.*  
*Exposed cable conduit.*

*He knows what he needs.*

*He reaches the junction. The bus is stopped dead. People are trapped. The street network is failing. A power relay is about to blow — if it does, it takes three more blocks with it.*

*Lydrix stands at the centre of it.*

*Takes a breath.*

*And goes in.*

**HYPERFOCUS MODE — ACTIVATES.**

*Sound design shift: the crowd noise drops to a whisper. The city's chaos becomes silence. The visual overlays go from red warnings to clean, glowing circuit-lines. Everything becomes a 3D map.*

*Lydrix's posture changes — tension drops out of his shoulders. In Hyperfocus Mode, this is where he's most himself. Most free.*

*He reaches out.*

*The scrap moves.*

*Pieces of metal lift, rotate, and lock together with mechanical precision. Cables snake and connect. The broken service droid's chassis disassembles and becomes a stabilisation rig.*

*In fifteen seconds, Lydrix has built a bypass relay from scratch.*

*He slots it into the junction.*

*SYSTEMS RESTORE. One by one — lights, bus doors, security doors, drones.*

*The crowd goes quiet.*

*Then murmuring. Then filming.*

---

### SCENE 7 — AFTERMATH

**EXT. THE CORE — JUNCTION — CONTINUOUS**

*Hyperfocus Mode fades. The world rushes back in.*

*Crowd noise. Screens. Smells. The absolute chaos of being a person in a city full of people.*

*Lydrix blinks. His hands are shaking slightly. He's exhausted — not physically, but somewhere deeper. The crash after the mode always feels like the world doubled in weight.*

*People are staring. Some are filming.*

**BYSTANDER 1**  
*(muttering)*  
Is that... is he modified?

**BYSTANDER 2**  
*(filming)*  
Did you see that? He just — he just built that—

**BYSTANDER 3**  
*(nervous)*  
Someone should call Grid Watch.

*Lydrix hears that last one. He puts his earphone back in. Pulls his jacket collar up.*

*And walks.*

---

### SCENE 8 — STATIC KING WATCHES

**INT. THE UNDERCROFT — SIGNAL QUARTER — SAME TIME**

*The Static watch their feeds.*

*Glitch has pulled up every camera angle on Lydrix. She's rewinding, replaying, zooming in.*

**GLITCH**  
Oh that is *interesting.*

**BYTEWITCH**  
Technopath. Advanced. The bypass he built — that's not amateur hour.

**OVERCLOCK**  
So? We just hit him harder next time.

*Static King holds up one hand. Overclock goes quiet.*

**STATIC KING**  
He built that in fifteen seconds. From scrap. While in a crowd.  
*(pause)*  
And then he just... walked away.  
*(beat)*  
No celebration. No fear. Like it was nothing.

*He stands. Moves toward the screen. Studies Lydrix's face.*

**STATIC KING**  
Find out who he is. Where he goes. Who knows him.  
*(quiet)*  
He's not an obstacle. Not yet.  
But if we leave him alone — he becomes one.

*Null slides out of the shadows without a word. Already on it.*

## ACT THREE — NEW HOME, RISING THREAT

---

### SCENE 9 — KAT SEES HIM

**EXT. THE SPRAWL — MAKE IT WORK — AFTERNOON**

*Bolt is back from the Eastside run, rolling past a junction in The Sprawl — old non-Grid camera on a wall above him. He glances up at it out of habit. The camera catches something.*

*Later — Kat is watching the shaky footage on an old tablet (no Grid connection, local storage only). Lydrix at the junction. The scrap rising. The relay snapping into place.*

*Kat watches it three times.*

**KAT**  
*(quietly, to herself)*  
Well. That's someone worth knowing.

*Bolt is next to her, watching too.*

**BOLT**  
He appears to have no fixed address. Signal trace returns nothing. He is effectively off-Grid by accident.

**KAT**  
How do you know that?

**BOLT**  
I notice things.

*Kat raises an eyebrow but doesn't push it.*

---

### SCENE 10 — THE OFFER

**EXT. THE SPRAWL — OUTSIDE MAKE IT WORK — DUSK**

*Lydrix is drifting. Earphones in. He's walking with nowhere to go — the crash after Hyperfocus Mode leaves him directionless, like someone pulled the map out of his head.*

*He ends up in The Sprawl without meaning to. Quieter here. Less Grid density. His shoulders drop slightly.*

*He passes a workshop. Warm light in the windows. Hand-painted signs. Bolt is outside, mid-delivery argument with a customer over a package.*

*And in the doorway — Kat.*

*Paint on her face. Tool in hand. She looks at Lydrix like she already knows exactly who he is.*

**KAT**  
You can use the storage space upstairs.

*Lydrix stops. Pulls one earphone out.*

**LYDRIX**  
I'm not moving in.

**KAT**  
Sure, mate.

*She turns and walks inside. Like it's already decided. Like the conversation is over.*

*Lydrix looks at the shop. Then at the city behind him — the noise, the screens, the Grid that never stops demanding things.*

*He looks back at the warm light in the window.*

*For the first time today, he stops walking.*

*He does not leave.*

---

### SCENE 11 — WORKSHOP CHAOS (COMEDY BUTTON)

**INT. MAKE IT WORK — EVENING**

*Lydrix is inside now. He's standing near a workbench, slightly rigid, taking in the environment.*

*The workshop is extraordinary. Kat has a system — she swears she has a system — but from the outside it looks like a very productive explosion.*

*Lydrix's photographic memory is cataloguing everything. Every tool. Every shelf. Every stray piece of glass.*

*He quietly moves a wrench from the floor to its correct hook on the wall.*

*Kat doesn't notice. She's mid-job, looking for something.*

**KAT**  
Where's the angled pry? I had it literally two minutes ago—

**LYDRIX**  
*(without looking up)*  
Left side of the second bench. Under the copper tubing. It's been there for three hours.

*Kat stares at him.*

**KAT**  
How do you know that?

**LYDRIX**  
I remember where things are.

*Beat.*

**KAT**  
All things?

**LYDRIX**  
All things.

*Kat retrieves the pry. Gets back to work. A pause.*

*Lydrix quietly begins reorganising a shelf of fasteners. By size. By type. Alphabetically within type.*

*Bolt watches this from the charging corner with enormous round eyes. Says nothing.*

*Several minutes pass. Then—*

**KAT**  
*(not looking up)*  
Are you tidying my workshop?

**LYDRIX**  
I'm organising a system.

**KAT**  
I HAD A SYSTEM.

**LYDRIX**  
Your system was chaos.

**KAT**  
IT WAS MY CHAOS. I KNEW WHERE EVERYTHING WAS.

**LYDRIX**  
You just asked me where the pry was.

*Beat.*

*Kat opens her mouth. Closes it.*

*Bolt makes a tiny sound. Not quite a laugh. Definitely a laugh.*

**KAT**  
*(pointing at Bolt)*  
Don't.

**BOLT**  
I said nothing.

**KAT**  
*(pointing at Lydrix)*  
Leave the shelves. Touch the red toolbox and I will throw you back into The Core.

*Lydrix considers the red toolbox. Steps away from it. But he's already memorised exactly what's in it and how it should be sorted.*

*He doesn't say this out loud.*

*He does file it away for later.*

---

### SCENE 12 — VILLAIN TAG

**INT. THE UNDERCROFT — LATE NIGHT**

*The rest of The Static have gone. Just Static King and Null in the data room.*

*Null places a small device on the table — a compiled dossier. No words, just data.*

*Static King reviews it. Name: unknown. Origin: unknown. Grid record: minimal, patchy. No fixed address. No registered abilities.*

**STATIC KING**  
He's been invisible on purpose.  
*(beat)*  
Smart.

*He closes the dossier. Looks at the citywide map on the screens.*

**STATIC KING**  
The cascade today was a test. The Grid recovered in fifteen seconds because of one person.  
*(pause)*  
All future operations will account for him.  
*(quiet)*  
Find the place he stopped walking. Find the warm light.

*Null nods. Disappears into shadow.*

*Static King looks at the city.*

**STATIC KING**  
*(almost to himself)*  
You can't rebuild the world by fixing one junction at a time, kid.  
But I understand the instinct.

*The screens flicker.*

*FADE TO BLACK.*
