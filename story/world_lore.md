# Hyperfocus Mode — World Lore

---

## The World

The story takes place in **Nexus City** — a dense, loud, hyper-connected metropolis where technology runs absolutely everything. Transport, power, communication, security, food distribution, and even street lighting are all managed by one giant interconnected city system called **The Grid**.

The Grid is efficient, fast, and fragile.

Most people love it. Lydrix finds it overwhelming.

---

## The Grid

The Grid is the city's nervous system.

- Every device, vehicle, and building is connected
- Runs on a central server cluster buried deep under the city
- Citizens interact with it constantly through implants, devices, and smart surfaces
- Managed by a corporation called **Nexus Corp** who claim to keep the city running smoothly

The Grid is also what makes The Static so dangerous. If they can corrupt it, they can corrupt everything.

---

## Nexus Corp

The corporation that controls The Grid.

- Publicly presents as a city services provider
- Secretly monitors all data, communications, and movement
- Has its own private security force called **Grid Watch**
- Interested in Lydrix because a technopath who can rebuild systems is either a weapon or a threat

Nexus Corp may not be a direct villain in season one, but they feel like a shadow watching from above. Likely the bigger season two threat.

## The City Zones

| Zone | Description |
|---|---|
| The Core | Central city hub. Bright, fast, crowded, overwhelming. Where most Grid attacks happen. |
| The Sprawl | Outer residential areas. Messier, less polished, more community-driven. Where Kat's shop lives. |
| The Undercroft | Underground tunnels, old infrastructure, forgotten systems. Where The Static likely operate from. |
| The Signal Quarter | Tech district. Servers, labs, data centres. Where ByteWitch and Static King feel most at home. |
| The Dead Zones | Areas where The Grid signal is weak or absent. Rare, strange, and slightly dangerous. Null knows them all. |

---

## Kat's Workshop

Sits in The Sprawl. The one place in Nexus City that runs completely off-grid.

- Cash only
- Paper records
- No smart surfaces, no Grid connections, no digital systems
- Hackers cannot touch it because there is nothing for them to grab

The safest base in the whole city and the emotional heart of the story.

---

## Tech and Society

Most citizens of Nexus City are deeply dependent on The Grid:

- They pay digitally
- They travel by smart transit
- They communicate through Grid-linked devices
- When The Static attacks, normal life breaks down immediately

That dependency is the whole point of The Static's plan. They want to show people how fragile their world really is.

---

## Technopaths

Lydrix is rare but not completely alone.

- Technopaths are people born with an ability to sense and interact with systems
- Not fully understood by science or society
- Nexus Corp has been quietly tracking them
- The Static may also be hunting them — to recruit or eliminate

This opens future story threads where Lydrix discovers others like him, building toward a wider conflict.

---

## World Theme

Nexus City represents a world that built itself around efficiency and connection but forgot to ask whether everyone actually wanted to be connected.

For Lydrix, the city is both a constant attack on his senses and the thing he has the most power over. That tension is what drives the story.
