# HYPERFOCUS MODE
### Animated Series — Pitch One-Pager

> *"Being different is not the problem. The world not understanding you is."*

---

## THE LOGLINE

A neurodivergent technopath who can build anything from anything must protect a city under siege from elite hackers — while learning that the hardest system to rebuild is himself.

---

## THE WORLD

**Nexus City.** A dense, neon, hyper-connected metropolis where The Grid runs everything — transport, power, doors, drones, daily life. For most people, it's invisible infrastructure. For **Lydrix**, it's a constant roar of signals he can't turn off.

The Grid is efficient. It is also fragile. And someone has found the cracks.

---

## THE CHARACTERS

**LYDRIX** — Early 20s. Neurodivergent technopath. Sees the hidden systems inside matter and machines. Can dismantle and rebuild technology with his hands and his mind. When he drops into **Hyperfocus Mode**, the world goes silent, patterns light up, and he can reconstruct failing city infrastructure in seconds. The cost: total mental and physical crash afterwards. He's been invisible on purpose his whole life. That's about to stop working.

**KAT** — Workshop owner. Bold, blunt, deeply practical. Runs **Make It Work** on cash and paper records — zero Grid connection, untouchable by hackers. Multi-craft maker: painting, 3D printing, glass blowing, repairs, prototypes. Never says no to a job she's never done before. Figures it out as she goes. Offers Lydrix the storage space upstairs with zero fuss. Changes his life.

**BOLT** — Self-modified delivery droid. Came in with a broken wheel and never left. Runs on older firmware The Static can't hack. Delivers packages with life-or-death seriousness. Puts a blanket on Kat when she falls asleep in her chair. Secretly agrees with Lydrix about the workshop chaos. Will never say this out loud.

**THE STATIC** — Organised hacker crew. Five members: **Static King** (system dominance), **Glitch** (signal chaos), **ByteWitch** (code possession), **Null** (total erasure), **Overclock** (brute force augmentation). They aren't destroying the city for money. They want to collapse The Grid and rebuild the world on their terms. The dark mirror of everything Lydrix is.

---

## THE STORY

The Static trigger a city-level Grid cascade. Lydrix saves eight million people from disaster in fifteen seconds using scrap from the street.

Then he walks away. Exhausted. Directionless. The crash hitting hard.

Kat saw the footage. She offers him the storage space upstairs like it's nothing.

*"I'm not moving in."*  
*"Sure, mate."*

He doesn't leave.

Make It Work becomes the base. The Static escalate. Nexus Corp's security arm, **Grid Watch**, starts investigating the junction incident. And Lydrix, for the first time in his life, has people worth fighting for — which means he can no longer afford to stay hidden.

## WHY THIS STORY. WHY NOW.

Neurodivergent characters in animation are rarely the hero. When they appear, they're a quirk, a side note, a lesson for someone else.

**Hyperfocus Mode** puts neurodivergence at the centre — not as a flaw to overcome, but as the source of genuine, extraordinary power. Lydrix's ADHD-coded hyperfocus, his sensory processing, his pattern recognition — these are not obstacles. They are his superpower.

This is the anime for every kid who was told their brain was broken.

It isn't. The system is.
