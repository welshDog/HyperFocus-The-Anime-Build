# Bolt — Character Sheet

**Type:** Self-modified delivery droid  
**Role:** Shop delivery, messenger, scout, emotional heart of Make It Work  
**Status:** Nobody hired him. He just never left.

---

## Origin

Bolt showed up at Make It Work one day with a broken wheel and a package to deliver. Kat fixed the wheel. Bolt never left.

Nobody knows exactly where Bolt came from. The serial number is scratched off. The manufacturer logo is painted over with a small hand-painted lightning bolt, which is how the name stuck.

---

## Look

- Small to medium height, roughly human shaped but clearly mechanical
- Mismatched parts from different eras of tech — one arm looks newer than the other
- Battered delivery satchel permanently strapped across one shoulder
- Scratched and dented casing with patches of bright paint from Kat's workshop
- Two big round expressive optical eyes that show emotion like an old cartoon character
- Small speaker grille where a mouth would be
- Moves fast but with a slight wobble on the left side that never got properly fixed
- Small lightning bolt painted on chest by Kat — Bolt loves it

**Colours:** Worn silver and white base, splashes of yellow and orange from Kat's paint, scuffed boots painted to match.

---

## Personality

- Enthusiastic to an almost alarming degree
- Takes every delivery extremely seriously like it is a life or death mission
- Incredibly loyal to Kat and quietly in awe of Lydrix
- Talks in short clipped sentences but with huge emotional range
- Gets genuinely offended if a delivery is refused or a customer is rude
- Has strong opinions about organisation and secretly agrees with Lydrix about the workshop chaos
- Will never say this in front of Kat

---

## Speech Style

Short punchy bursts with occasional technical glitches mid-sentence:

- "Package. Delivered. On time. As always."
- "Customer was rude. I noted it. In my log."
- "Kat said yes to the job. I have concerns. Running calculations."
- "Lydrix. The workshop. It is tidy. I approve— I mean. Hello."
- "Delivery complete. Bolt out."

---

## Special Ability

Bolt runs on older firmware that predates modern Grid connectivity.

- The Static cannot fully hack or track Bolt
- Modern attack code does not recognise Bolt's systems properly
- Makes Bolt the perfect messenger and scout during city attacks
- Lydrix quietly upgraded Bolt's systems without telling anyone
- Bolt knows. Bolt says nothing. They have an understanding.

---

## Role in the Story

- Delivers materials, parts, and supplies for Kat's jobs
- Carries messages when the Grid is down
- Gets into trouble in the city and has to be rescued
- Accidentally delivers things to the wrong people at the worst moment
- Witnesses everything and says nothing until the worst possible moment
- Becomes surprisingly important because Bolt goes everywhere and sees everything

---

## Bolt and Lydrix

- Lydrix respects Bolt because Bolt is logical, precise, and doesn't ask unnecessary questions
- Bolt is fascinated by Lydrix's ability to rebuild systems from nothing
- They communicate mostly in silence, technical gestures, and the occasional nod
- Bolt would take a hit for Lydrix without hesitation
- Lydrix would rebuild Bolt from scratch without being asked

---

## Bolt and Kat

- Kat treats Bolt like a mate, not a machine
- Bolt finds this confusing and wonderful in equal measure
- Kat forgets Bolt is a droid constantly and apologises for it constantly
- Bolt pretends not to notice — Bolt notices everything
- When Kat falls asleep on the chair, Bolt quietly puts a blanket over her and gets back to work

---

## Best Scene

Bolt arrives back from a delivery, slightly singed and missing a panel on one arm.

**KAT:** What happened?!

**BOLT:** Delivery complete. Minor complications.

**KAT:** You're on fire!

**BOLT:** *(looks at arm)* Noted. Non-critical.

**LYDRIX:** *(already walking over with tools)* Sit down.

**BOLT:** I have two more deliveries.

**LYDRIX:** Sit. Down.

**BOLT:** ...Sitting down.

---

## Why Bolt Works

- Comic relief without being annoying
- Emotionally resonant without being dramatic
- Practically useful in the plot
- Unhackable by The Static — strategically important
- The quiet heart of the workshop

Bolt is the character everyone loves by episode three without knowing exactly when it happened.
